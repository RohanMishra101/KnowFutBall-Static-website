Let's Know Football

A popular team sport across the world, football is played between two teams of eleven players each. It is played on a rectangular field with a goal at either end using a spherical ball. The purpose of the game is to score by placing the ball in the goal of the other team, mostly using the feet, however, other body parts—aside from the hands and arms—may also be utilized. After the game, the side with the most goals wins.

Timeline

1863

Codification of Rules :

The Football Association (FA), established in England in 1863, established the rules of modern football. This event was significant because it established the boundaries between rugby and football and helped to codify the rules that would later form the foundation of the game.

1872

First International Match :

Scotland and England played their first-ever official international football match on November 30, 1872, at Glasgow, Scotland. Because it was the first time the game was played between national teams, this match is important because it opened up international competition.

1904

Formation of FIFA :

May 21, 1904 saw the founding of the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) in Paris. Overseeing international events such as the World Cup, this international football regulatory body would go on to become one of the most powerful sports organizations globally.

1930

First FIFA World Cup :

Uruguay hosted the first FIFA World Cup in 1930. This was the first international football event with competing nations from all around the world. When Uruguay emerged victorious in the competition, it paved the way for what would grow to be the most esteemed occasion in international football.

1992

UEFA Champions League Formation :

The European Cup, which was the name of the UEFA Champions League until the 1991-1992 season, was renamed and enlarged in 1992. This metamorphosis signified a momentous change in European club football. Due to the Champions League's new format, which permitted multiple entries from certain nations, elite European teams participated more frequently.

Iconic Players

Pelé

The recognizable forward from Brazil represents the pinnacle of football. Acclaimed for his agility, strong headers, and prolific goal scoring, he was a key player in Brazil's three World Cup championships (1958, 1962, 1970) that set records. Though his professional accomplishments are unmatched, his influence goes much beyond the numbers. As the person who popularized the sport and inspired millions of people worldwide, Pelé is a global icon. Despite his passing in 2022, his reputation as one of the all-time great football players is still deeply ingrained in the memories of both players and spectators.

Lionel Messi

Born in Rosario, Argentina, on June 24, 1987, Lionel Messi is widely regarded as one of the best football players of all time. He started his career at FC Barcelona and worked his way up to become a key member of the squad. Messi became an incredible talent throughout his 20 years at Barcelona, renowned for his flawless dribbling, outstanding vision, and remarkable goal-scoring ability. In2022, Messi fulfilled a long-held ambition of his by helping Argentina win their first-ever Copa America championship. Messi was instrumental in Argentina's historic World Cup triumph in 2022, which saw him return the prized trophy to his native country for the first time since 1986.

Diego Maradona

On the pitch, the legendary Argentine player was unstoppable. His intense zeal drove his amazing dribbling abilities, captivating control, and accurate passing. He made history in football when he led Argentina to the World Cup in 1986 on his own. Despite the controversies that dogged his career, he still has the unrivaled ability to motivate his squad and enthrall the audience with his charismatic leadership. Regretfully, Maradona went away in 2020, but not before leaving a legacy that goes beyond football and still motivating fans and athletes alike.
